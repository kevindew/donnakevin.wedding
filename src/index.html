How to get there

<%= svgCore.icon({ iconName: 'car' }, { classes: ['icon-guide__icon'] }).html %>

Car

Car is the easiest way to get to Rudding Park and there is plenty of parking at the venue. Rudding park is just off the A658. Take J47 off the A1 onto A59 to reach the A658. The venue is really well signposted.

<%= svgCore.icon({ iconName: 'subway' }, { classes: ['icon-guide__icon'] }).html %>

Public transport

The nearest station to Rudding Park is Panal and it is just a 5 minute taxi ride from there.

Alternatively you can head to Harrogate station, the venue is a 13 minute taxi ride from there.

We have found that booking trains in advance is much cheaper, especially if you are travelling from London. Book your tickets here: lner.co.uk

<%= svgCore.icon({ iconName: 'taxi' }, { classes: ['icon-guide__icon'] }).html %>

Local Taxis

There are no public transport links to the venue itself from Harrogate or Pannal so we strongly suggest that you pre-book your taxi for the day/evening in advance. We have been recommended Blue Line taxis, to book please call 01423 530830. They usually cost around £10 each way.

Places to stay

Rudding Park

Stay with us at Rudding Park Hotel & Spa and take advantage of the on-site golf and Spa facilities, find out more about the venue at ruddingpark.co.uk The rooms here are slightly on the more expensive side but it is ultra convenient and you will be able to have breakfast with us the morning after the big day - if you haven’t had enough of us that is! Let us know if you decide to stay here so we can organise a private room for breakfasts. Rooms here start from around £217 (though you can get slightly cheaper if you book last minute) and are available to book now.

Premier Inn Harrogate South

There is a Premier Inn 10 minutes away by car which is perfect to stay at if you are driving and don’t want to stay at the venue or drive through the centre of Harrogate. Everyone knows what to expect with a Premier Inn and they do great breakfasts too. You can expect rooms here to cost around £54 a night and are available to book now.

Harrogate Town Centre

If you are travelling by train or would prefer more of a city break then staying in the centre of Harrogate would be the perfect option. If you have never travelled to the north before then Harrogate is a real treat! It is a picturesque town full of lovely shops and places to eat - including the famous Betty’s Tea Rooms. Harrogate has a heritage as a fashionable spa town so there’s lots to see and do, find out more here visitharrogate.co.uk . There are lots of hotel and Airbnb options available and it’s probably best to start your search here booking.com .

On the day

11:00 Ceremony

Please start arriving from 10:00, we would like all bums in seats before 10:45 ready for the big moment.

11:30 Reception

Time to throw some confetti (if the weather is nice) and drink Prosecco! We will have a classical guitarist to keep us entertained too - see if you can spot which song we have specially requested for him to play. There will also be a kids room full of things to keep the little ones entertained.

13:30 Breakfast

We should all be a little bit tipsy and very hungry at this point so let’s sit and enjoy some delicious food. There will be some time after the meal where you can freshen up or carry on drinking until the party starts in the evening.

18:30 Party

Witness the cutting of the cake and our first dance. A DJ will encourage everyone to hit the dance floor and throw some shapes.

20:00 Buffet

Everyone should have the munchies by now, we will be providing some finger food for everyone and there will also be tonnes of cake.

01:00 Home time

The party will go on until 1am and if you have made it to this time well done, we will probably be fast asleep at this point. If you are staying at the venue there is another residents bar that will be open if you want to carry on celebrating otherwise if you have booked your taxi it will be waiting outside for you. Don’t forget the clocks for forward an hour at 02:00!
